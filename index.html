When I was younger I always wanted to be a Chef despite the late night, and long weekends. Shortly after my son was born my wife was still working nights at a hospital so ended up getting trapped in my position as a prep cook and found it difficult to move up without being able to work nights, and started driving for Uber part time on weekends when my mother could baby sit for us, so we could both be at work.

While this helped get by it didn't exactly enhance our lives completely, but it did make me realize how much technology and the people building could help so many people everywhere. As a Chef at a restaurant you can only benefit the people in your nearby community but helping develop web applications you connect people around the world. From airbnb helping people rent out their spare rooms to make extra money, or essentially start new business venture renting owned properties, to apps that allow you to sell your used goods to others.
